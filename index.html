Hacking & Piracy in India

by Shaan Shiv Suleman & Aditya Shankar


Introduction to hacking & piracy in India

Ankit Fadia: Ankit is not new to the world of hacking and is one of the most famous personality in the in the field of ethical hacking. He is a world renowned hacker and has written over 10 books in the field of computer engineering and hacking. He is based out of the Silicon Valley in California.
Rahul Tyagi: One of India’s renowned ethical hacker Rahul Tyagi, is a post-graduate major in computer science, an Actor and an Author who has written two books Hacking Crux 1 and 2, both the books are on information security. These two books are highly informative and used by many professionals and colleges as a reference to ethical hacking. He has conducted over a hundred training sessions globally in the past 5 years.
Koushik Dutta: He is an ex employee of Microsoft; actually did his internship with them and left the company to hack android cell phones. He is presently working with Clockwork mod and is responsible for Clockeworkmod recovery and ROM Manager for Android rooting and the core member of famed UnrEVOked team.
Trishneet Arora: The 19 year old boy is internationally acclaimed hacker, speaker, consultant, author and Entrepreneur. He wrote the book ‘The Hacking Era’ and is the Chief Technical Officer at his company called TAC security solutions. His diverse portfolio in consulting, speaking to It and Educational clients gives him a more practical experience of real world threat than most professionals.
Vivek Ramachandran: A world renowned security researcher and evangelist is expertise in computer and network security, wireless security, computer forensics, embedded systems security and e-governance. He authored the book “wireless Penetration test using Backtrack” and “The Metasploit Megaprimer”.


Brief timeline of hacking & piracy in India

1995 May: The Indian Copyright Act (1995) confers copyright on 
(i) Original literary, dramatic, musical and artistic works, 
(ii) Cinematographic films and
(iii) sound recordings. The word `original’ means that it should not be copied from other works or alternatively it should be the outcome of independent efforts. 
The Act empowers copyright holder(s) to do or authorise doing a number of activities. The important among these are:
To reproduce the work in material form
To publish the work
To perform the work in public or communicate it to the public
To produce, reproduce, perform or publish any translation of the work
To make any cinematographic film or a record in respect of the work
To make any adaptation of the work
To do, in relation to a translation or an adaptation of the work, any of the acts specified to the work in sub clauses to (a) to (f)

2009 October: MTV India announced the launch of Ankit Fadia's new TV show on MTV called MTV What the Hack!, where Fadia gave tips on how to make good use of the Internet and answers people's questions. Internet users could email their problems to MTV India and Fadia gave them a solution on the show.

2010 September: Savukku (English: whip) is an anonymous, non-profit, whistle blowing website. The site publishes articles in Tamil and English. It is known for leaking sensational 2G spectrum scam taped conversation and publishing articles on corrupt government employees, politicians, judges, journalists and socio-political affairs.

2010 December 3: The first Malware Conference, MALCON takes place in India. Founded by Rajshekhar Murthy, Malware coders are invited to showcase their skills at this annual event supported by the Government of India. An advanced malware for Symbian OS is released by hacker A0drul3z.

2012 July 12: Indian Government suffered one of the biggest cyber attack in the country's history. Hackers managed to compromise more than 10,000 email address of top government officials. The cybercriminals managed to steal email IDs belong to official working at the Prime Minister's office, Defence, external affairs, finance ministries and Intelligence agencies.

2013: A new study recently pointed out that online piracy levels in India remained at 60 per cent, with nearly $2.9 billion of unlicensed software being installed.

2013: According to non-profit organisation BSA, India is second only to China (over $8.7 billion) in the Asia Pacific region in terms of commercial value of unlicensed software sold.

2014 February 28: The Madras High Court ordered the Joint Secretary, Cyber Law Division of Union Department of Information Technology to block Savukku within ten days and called other affected individuals to lodge separate police cases, on hearing the writ petition filed by Mahalakshmi.

Date unknown: “The Special 301 Report again lists India as a priority watch nation. Despite a large domestic creative industry in film, music and other copyright intensive industries, India continues to lag badly in both the legal framework for IP protection,” the report said.
